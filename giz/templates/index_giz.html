About IOED

Strategic Planning & Implementing Unit (SPIU) of Environment Protection Department (EPD) Punjab is mandated to execute the Punjab Green Development Program (PGDP) and other initiatives of the department. The directorate of Environmental Policy and Planning (EPP) at SPIU has a function to “Compile and organize environmental data collected by others and work with those who collect the data and interpret it for informed decision making in environmental protection sector in the province”.


Deutsche Gesellschaft für Internationale Zusammenarbeit (GIZ) GmbH is executing the "Improvement of labour and environmental standards in Pakistan's textile industry (TextILES)" project. GIZ’s TextILES project is approached by SPIU for assistance in the context of preparation of inventory of those organizations which are collecting and analyzing environmental data.


The goal is to strengthen the avenue of informed decision making and to strategize the policy goals/ priority areas of intervention for environment protection in the province, as environment is a cross cutting subject. Project, in the long run, aims at bringing stakeholders together for integrated and cohesive actions to address the current needs pertaining to the strengthening of Environmental Standards in all sectors, especially the Textiles. Hence, this project was conceived.

Objective is to strengthen environmental governance through:

  • Evaluating various organizations for presence of environmental data;
  • Understanding the methodology/procedure adopted for collection of environmental data by these organizations;
  • Identification of the bottlenecks in sharing the environmental data with the EPD;
  • Presenting the data of one to two organizations on the dashboard;
  • Recommending the way forward for strengthening informed decision making.

Findings

The study conclude that largest number of organizations are dealing with “Water” followed by Air, Soil, Forest, Wildlife, Fisheries and Solid Waste (Fig-1). These organizations were further grouped with reference to the sub-component of Environment (Fig-2). The Data Collection frequency in terms of daily, monthly, annually, project and research based has also been evaluated (Fig-3). The constraints in terms of Structural, Legislative, HR & Financial (Fig-4) as well as response of organizations regarding constraints has also been recorded (Fig-5).
PCRWR monitors the water quality of surface water bodies (Fig-6). This data indicates the quality of various water bodies in accordance with the permissible limits (Fig-7).

Frequently Asked Questions

For answer, please click on the question.

  • When study was initiated and completed?

    This study was initiated during December, 2022 and was completed during August, 2023, respectively.

  • Who conducted this study?

    The study was conducted by M/s Earthcon Management & Development Solutions (Pvt.) Ltd.

  • What are the limitations to the study?

    Limitations are as follows:

    1. There may be other departments having environmental data in Punjab, but this study is based on some limited number of organizations.
    2. Information collected from organizations, through various means (including Google forms, meetings, telephone calls and emails), has been incorporated in the report.
    3. The data collection exercise was limited to the certain components of environment.
    4. The quantitative data displayed on the dashboard was limited to only one organization.
  • What sort of Government Departments were included in this study?

    This study includes the Government departments, their attached departments as well as Autonomous Bodies & Companies.

  • What is meant by the working scope for this study?

    The working scope of organizations having environmental data is divided into environmental monitoring, research, biodiversity and policy.
