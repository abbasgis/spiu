Strategic Planning and Implementation Unit (SPIU)
Punjab Green Development Program (PGDP)
Environment Protection Department, Punjab

Punjab Green Development Program is a World Bank funded program of worth US$ 273 Million. The share of World Bank and Government of the Punjab is US$ 200 Million and US$ 73 Million respectively. The key objectives of PGDP are (a) Strengthening Environmental Governance and (b) Promoting Green Investments. The Implementing Agencies of this program are Environment Department, Industries Department, Energy Department, Transport Department, Finance Department and P&D Department.

WHO WE ARE

The SPIU at the EPD will have overall responsibility for monitoring and reporting on the Program’s results. It will conduct regular M&E reporting on progress in each of the results areas, including the collection of output milestones for intermediate indicators and DLIs. A private firm will be competitively recruited by GoPunjab to conduct independent verification of achievement of the different targets set under each DLI.

The SPIU will support the EPD, the Project Coordination Unit, and the PSC to monitor overall Program implementation, including the application of E&S safeguard measures according to domestic requirements. Each implementing agency will use its existing institutional set-up to monitor the implementation of its respective Program activities. The SPIU will regularly collect M&E information from the implementing agencies and compile Program-level M&E reports for review by the Program Coordination Unit, the PSC, and the World Bank.

The SPIU at the EPD will be responsible for preparing programlevel annual consolidated financial statements, which will be audited by the Department of the Auditor General of Pakistan. Audit reports will be submitted to the World Bank within six months after the close of each financial year.

Our Departments


Environment Protection Department, Punjab

    EPD is an implementation agency for the following DLI:
  • DLI-1: Improving EPD Capacity.
  • DLI-2: Air and Water Quality Monitoring.
  • DLI-3: Disclosure of Environmental Information and Citizen Engagement.
  • DLI-4: Sound Management of Plastics.
  • DLI-8: Mobilization of Sustainable Green Financing
  • For DLI-8, Environment Department along Finance Department will act as implementing agency

    Energy Department is an implementation agency. Their tasks include:
  • DLI-5: Energy Efficiency Investments in Public Institutions

Transport Department, Government of Punjab

    Transport Department is an implementation agency. Their tasks include:
  • DLI-6: Vehicle Compliance with Emission Standards

Industries Department GOVERNMENT OF PUNJAB

    Industries Department is an implementation agency. Their tasks include:
  • DLI-7: Green Investments by Polluting Industries.
